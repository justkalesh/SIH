Why Recycling Matters

Recycling is the process of converting waste materials into new materials and objects. It's an alternative to conventional waste disposal that can save material and help lower greenhouse gas emissions.

What Can Be Recycled?

Not all materials are recyclable. Here's a quick guide:

  • Paper: Newspapers, magazines, office paper
  • Cardboard: Boxes, packaging (flattened)
  • Plastics: Bottles, containers (check local codes)
  • Glass: Bottles and jars (clear, green, brown)
  • Metals: Aluminum cans, tin cans
Always rinse containers before recycling to avoid contamination.

The Recycling Process

Recycling involves several steps:

  1. Collection: Materials are collected from homes and businesses
  2. Processing: Materials are sorted, cleaned, and prepared
  3. Manufacturing: Recycled materials are made into new products
  4. Purchase: Consumers buy products made from recycled materials

Reduce and Reuse First

Remember that recycling is just one part of the waste hierarchy. Before recycling, consider:

  • Reducing your consumption
  • Reusing items when possible
  • Repairing broken items instead of replacing them
